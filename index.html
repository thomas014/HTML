Background

Abdomen AP, KUB, and pelvis AP radiographs are crucial for diagnosis and account for a significant portion of radiographic examinations.

  • High frequency of requests with repeated exposures may lead to cumulative radiation dose concerns.
  • Risk of stochastic cancer development from repeated exposures.
Slide 3

Background (Continued)

The audit seeks to evaluate DAP across different machines, benchmark results against the UK national DRLs, and support the advancement of radiation safety.

Key Objective

Ensure patient radiation doses remain as low as reasonably achievable (ALARA) while maintaining diagnostic quality across all digital radiography machines.

Context: Plain Radiographic Machines (TMH Gen4, TMH GEN5, TMH ACC3, TMH ACC4 & POH GEN2) have been replaced in recent years.

Comparison Across Exam Rooms

Observations

  • Significant variation exists between specific rooms.
  • High Outlier: TMH ACC2 (Mean DAP: 8.36).
  • Low Outlier: POH GEN1 (Mean DAP: 2.21).
  • POH A&E: High volume (1092 cases) with high DAP (~7.79).
Slide 9

Comparison: New vs. Old Machines

General Trend

Overall, the newly replaced machines demonstrate lower DAP values compared to the unreplaced (old) machines.

  • New machines (Gen4/5) leverage better detector sensitivity (DQE).
  • Old machines (e.g., POH GEN1, TSH GEN1) show higher variability.
  • Exceptions exist where older machines (like POH GEN1) show very low dose, possibly due to aggressive dose-saving protocols or calibration differences.
Slide 10

Discussion - Sampling & Volume

Sampling Period Recommendations

  • Issue: Insufficient sample sizes in TMH ACC3, TMH ACC4, and POH GEN2.
  • Cause: Recent commissioning (March 2025).
  • Action: Extend sampling period to at least six months.

Volume Influence

  • Issue: High-volume rooms (POH A&E and TSH GEN2) dominate the dataset.
  • Impact: POH A&E’s high DAP skews the overall NTWC result.
Slide 11

Discussion - Technical Factors

kVp Settings (TMH ACC4)

Observation: kVp settings in TMH ACC4 are higher (~85kVp) compared to other rooms (75-78kVp).

Result: Higher EI, but DAP remains comparable.

High kVp Analysis

  • Advantages: Increased penetration; better DQE.
  • Disadvantages: Reduced image contrast (higher scatter).

Recommendation: Investigate if high kVp protocols should be adopted for other machines utilizing virtual grids.

Slide 12

Limitations

  • Retrospective Nature: Unreported machine errors may skew results.
  • Patient Data: Patient weight was not recorded.
  • Image Quality: Not directly measured (only Exposure Index available).
  • Collimation: Collimation size was not recorded.
Slide 13

Follow Up Actions

POH A&E Investigation

  • Finding: DAP in POH A&E is higher than TMH & TSW.
  • Root Cause: Protocols differ.
    • POH A&E: ECF 0
    • POH GEN 1/2: ECF -3
  • Action: Review protocols to explore if lower ECF settings are feasible.

Specific Anomalies

  • TMH ACC2: High DAP observed; may result from machine aging.
Slide 14

Conclusion

  • The local DRL of NTWC DR is significantly lower than the UK standard (2019).
  • TMH demonstrates the lowest hospital DRL within the NTWC cluster.
  • Overall, the new machines have lower DAP than the old machines.
  • Variances in specific rooms likely attributed to machine aging or higher ECF settings.
